"What do you use for writing?" The answers are all over the place.

A strategy from a course. A graphic organizer from TPT. A framework someone shared in a Facebook group. Most practitioners who teach reading explicitly are piecing writing instruction together from fragments, and spending a lot of time doing it.

SSW exists because writing instruction deserves the same rigour as reading instruction. Explicit. Sequential. Every skill taught before the next one is introduced. A roadmap from the very first sentence to a finished essay.

What's in Foundations

4 units. Every skill taught explicitly before the next one is introduced. Students start where they are and move forward when they're genuinely ready.

1

Building Basic Sentences

Capitals, punctuation, nouns, and verbs. The foundations of a complete sentence. Students who have never been taught to write a sentence explicitly start here.

Sentence structure Nouns & verbs Capitalisation Punctuation
2

Expanding Sentences

Adjectives and adverbs. Making sentences more precise, more interesting, and more specific. Students learn to add detail without losing structure.

Adjectives Adverbs Sentence expansion
3

Sentence Variety and Types

Simple, compound, and complex sentences. Varying beginnings and structure. Students build a repertoire they can draw on intentionally, not by accident.

Simple sentences Compound sentences Complex sentences Sentence openers
4

Advanced Sentence Structure

Complex sentences, appositives, and the introduction of SPARK+SORT for idea generation. By the end of Unit 4, students are ready to move into paragraph writing.

Appositives SPARK+SORT Idea generation Paragraph readiness

Students learn each tool once. Then they grow with it across every unit, every genre, every level. The framework never changes. The writing does.

SPARK+SORT Idea generation

Solve the blank page problem

Before a student writes a single word, they use SPARK to pour out every idea freely, then SORT to choose their strongest ideas and land on a clear focus. The blank page stops being the problem.

STEMS Paragraph structure

Build every paragraph

Every sentence in a paragraph has a name and a job. Students learn STEMS once and use it across every unit. The framework doesn't change. The writing grows.

CAMPS Editing

Catch every error

A systematic editing process students internalise rather than guess at. Capitalisation, Agreement, Meaning, Punctuation, Spelling. In order, every time.

CHECKLISTS Intro & conclusion

Open and close with confidence

Introduction and conclusion checklists make the hardest paragraphs learnable and repeatable. Students check every element before moving on.
